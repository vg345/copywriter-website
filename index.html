Why is copywriting important?

You're here, which means you have something awesome to sell. Apparently, so do billions of other people. If your customer is a stranger just browsing through the internet, how do you set yourself apart from the competition? Because the competition isn't just for the product you're offering. It's also for your customer's attention. How do you get–and hold–the attention of the people you're selling to?

Now, remember, these people have never heard of you before. They don't know what you're offering. They don't trust you. They have no reason to pick you over someone else, someone that they might personally know. You want them to buy from you, because A) your awesome product deserves to be shared and B) that's what pays the bills.

How do you turn strangers into a loyal customers?

Through copywriting.

In all likelihood, the first contact a stranger will have with you is through your website. So, that's one of the critical places where copywriting is used. Improving rank for search engines, getting the brand across, getting people to like and trust the brand. And getting email or newsletter sign-ups.

Email is another place doomed without copywriting. This is where you turn the stranger into a repeat customer.

Infographics, videos, podcasts, webinars, speeches, ebooks, white papers, case studies, you name it. If you want it to get you more leads, it can benefit from copywriting.

There are a lot of ways to get more customers. Figuring out which technique would help you the most is part of my job description.
